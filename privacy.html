Privacy Policy

DeskDuck · Last updated: July 1, 2026

DeskDuck is a small hardware-and-app product that helps you spend less time on distracting apps. This policy explains, in plain language, what information DeskDuck does and does not collect, and how it is used. We have deliberately built DeskDuck to collect as little as possible.

The short version: Your screen-time data never leaves your phone. DeskDuck only sends a simple "time's up" signal to your duck. We don't have accounts, we don't take payments, we don't run analytics, and we don't sell anything about you.

How DeskDuck works

You choose which apps you'd like to cut back on and set a time limit. Your iPhone watches your usage of those apps entirely on the device, using Apple's built-in Screen Time (DeviceActivity) system. When you go over your limit, your phone sends a simple signal over the internet to our server, which tells your physical DeskDuck to quack. That quack is the nudge to put your phone down.

What stays on your phone and never leaves it

  • Which apps you've chosen to monitor.
  • How much time you've spent in those apps.
  • Your app usage history of any kind.

This information is handled entirely on your device by Apple's Screen Time system. DeskDuck never receives it, never stores it, and never transmits it. We could not see your app usage even if we wanted to.

What is sent to our server

When you cross a time limit, or when someone sends a quack to your duck, the only information sent to our server is:

  • Your duck's ID (a random code assigned to the hardware, not to you personally).
  • A signal that a quack should play, and which type (a normal quack or a "thinking of you" quack sent by a friend).
  • For the "send a quack to a friend" feature, an identifier for the sender's device or duck, used only to prevent spam by limiting how many quacks can be sent per day.

No app names, no usage details, no personal information, and no identity are ever sent. The duck ID is tied to a piece of hardware, not to your name, email, or account — because DeskDuck has no accounts.

What our server stores

Our server temporarily holds only what's needed to deliver a quack: duck IDs, the time a duck last checked in, and short-term daily counts used to prevent quack spam. This information is minimal, is not linked to your identity, and is not used to build any profile of you.

Resetting your duck

You can reset your duck at any time, which gives it a brand-new ID. This is useful if you ever shared your duck's code and want to stop receiving quacks from someone — the old code stops working immediately.

The website and email updates

If you sign up on our website to hear about DeskDuck, we collect your email address for one purpose only: to send you product updates you've asked for. Email sign-ups are handled and stored through our form provider, Formspree. We don't sell, rent, or share your email address, and you can ask us to remove it at any time by contacting us.

What we don't do

  • We don't have user accounts, logins, or passwords.
  • We don't collect payment information.
  • We don't use analytics or tracking tools in the app or on the website.
  • We don't sell, rent, or trade any information about you.
  • We don't build advertising profiles.

Children

DeskDuck is not directed at children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect information from them. If you believe a child has provided us information (for example, an email address on our website), please contact us and we'll remove it.

Changes to this policy

As DeskDuck grows, this policy may be updated — for example, if we add new features. If we make meaningful changes, we'll update the date at the top and, where appropriate, let sign-ups know. Continued use of DeskDuck after changes means you accept the updated policy.
